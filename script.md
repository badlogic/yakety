# Yakety Promo Video Script

## Setting
80s aesthetic with diverse nerds, retro computers, neon colors, and classic 80s props.

## Scene 1: Voice Control Revolution
**Setting**: Young, blond hip 80s guy sits in a room with an 80s computer and Rubik's cube.  
**Line**: "No more typing, now I just talk to the computer!"

## Scene 2: Push Button Productivity  
**Setting**: Another guy sitting in front of the computer with a big red button.  
**Line**: "I just press a button, speak, and the computer does my work!"

## Scene 3: Privacy Power User
**Setting**: A woman with big 80s hair and neon windbreaker sits at a computer surrounded by "TOP SECRET" stamps and shredded paper. A poster on the wall shows a crossed-out cloud with "NO CLOUD" written underneath.  
**Line**: "Everything runs on MY computer! No big brother watching, no data leaving my machine. Total privacy, radical!"  
**Alternative line**: "My voice never leaves this room! Local processing, baby - no corporate overlords!"

## Scene 4: Hacker's Paradise
**Setting**: A guy with mullet and aviator sunglasses types furiously on a keyboard. The screen shows code scrolling Matrix-style. Post-it notes everywhere with commands like "yakety -> deploy", "voice -> git commit".  
**Line**: "I programmed it to understand 'Deploy the missiles' means 'git push to production!' Voice commands, custom scripts - the future is NOW!"  
**Alternative line**: "Just said 'computer, refactor this spaghetti' and BOOM - clean code! I can make it do ANYTHING!"

## Scene 5: Group Finale
**Setting**: A group of diverse 80s nerds stands in front of a wall of computers.  
**Line**: "Yakety! Voice coding done right. Tight!"

## Additional Scene Suggestions

### Alternative Scene 3: Security Conscious
**Setting**: A paranoid-looking guy in a tinfoil hat sits in a basement surrounded by locks, chains, and "KEEP OUT" signs on his computer.  
**Line**: "No internet required! My secrets stay secret. Even the CIA can't hear what I'm coding!"

### Alternative Scene 4: Power User
**Setting**: A woman wearing a "BASIC PROGRAMMER" t-shirt has multiple keyboards connected to one computer. A whiteboard behind her shows voice command mappings.  
**Line**: "I taught it my lingo! 'Yakety yak' compiles, 'don't talk back' runs the tests!"
